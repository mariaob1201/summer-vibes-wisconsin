Trip Planning Tips

Make the most of your Wisconsin summer

🕑

Best Time to Visit

Late June through August is peak season. July offers warmest lake temps (70°F+). Book accommodations 4–6 weeks ahead.

⚓

Renting a Boat (Lancha)

Most marinas rent pontoons, kayaks, and motorboats by the hour or day. Bring a valid ID and expect to pay $80–$200/day for pontoons.

👪

Going with Family

Pack sunscreen, life jackets for kids, and snacks. Many state parks have picnic areas with grills. Check if the beach has lifeguards.

🏝

Hiking Essentials

Wear layers — mornings near lakes can be cool. Bring bug spray (mosquitoes are real in WI). Download offline trail maps via AllTrails.

🎣

Fishing License

Wisconsin requires a fishing license for ages 16+. Buy online at wildlifelicense.com/wi — a 1-day license is about $10 for non-residents.

🌟

Hidden Gem Tip

Avoid the busiest spots on holiday weekends. Visit Tuesday–Thursday for quieter beaches, easier boat rentals, and better parking.
